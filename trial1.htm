Study title: Studying the impact of a new prediction explanation method on radiologist interpretations of neural network predictions

Background: The goal of a prediction explanation method is to identify features which are relevant to the prediction of a neural network and convey that information to the user.

Motivation: This study is to determine if a new prediction explanation method for neural networks allows users to identify false positive predictions better than traditional methods.

Instructions: Each link below corresponds to a single chest X-ray which has had a positive prediction made by a neural network. Method A corresponds to 3 traditional methods while method B corresponds to a new proposed method. More detailed explanations of the methods are available by clicking the "(What is this?)" link above an image.

In each of the following sections the images are predicted positive for the pathology listed. Each explanation method is generated to explain the prediction of a single pathology only by looking at what features the neural network used to make the prediction. Highlighting unrelated features, which may be present, would be a sign that incorrect features were used. Not all predictions are correct.
